Beef Bourguignon

Though it may seem time-consuming, all home cooks—from beginners to masters—should learn how to make a well-executed Beef Bourguignon

The dish originates from the Burgundy region (in French, Bourgogne) which is in the east of present-day France, as do many of the more well-known French dishes such as coq au vin, escargot, persillé ham, œufs meurette, gougères, pain d'épices, etc.

It is a stew prepared with beef braised in red wine, traditionally red Burgundy, and beef broth, generally flavoured with garlic, onions and a bouquet garni, with pearl onions and mushrooms added towards the end of cooking.

Ingredients

  •  1 tablespoon good olive oil
  •  8 ounces dry cured center cut applewood smoked bacon, diced
  •  2 1/2 pounds chuck beef cut into 1-inch cubes
  •  Kosher salt
  •  Freshly ground black pepper
  •   1 pound carrots, sliced diagonally into 1-inch chunks
  •   2 yellow onions, sliced
  •   2 teaspoons chopped garlic (2 cloves)
  •   1/2 cup Cognac
  •   1 (750 ml.) bottle good dry Pinot Noir
  •   1 can (2 cups) beef broth
  •   1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  •   4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  •   3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  •   1 pound frozen whole onions
  •   1 pound fresh mushrooms thickly sliced
  •  Sour Dough Toast rubbed with garlic
  •  1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley

Cooking Directions

When well-executed, Beef Bourguignon is a truly glorious dish. The techniques that govern the preparation of this dish do involve a few extra steps, but these are the details that ensure moist meat and a rich sauce, the signature of a superior Bourguignon.
  1. 1. Preheat the oven to 250 degrees F.

  2. 2. Heat the olive oil in a large Dutch oven.

  3. 3. Add the bacon and cook over medium heat for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the bacon is lightly browned.

  4. 4. Remove the bacon with a slotted spoon to a large plate.

  5. 5. Dry the beef cubes with paper towels and then sprinkle them with salt and pepper.

  6. 6. In batches in single layers, sear the beef in the hot oil for 3 to 5 minutes, turning to brown on all sides.

  7. 7. Remove the seared cubes to the plate with the bacon and continue searing until all the beef is browned.

  8. 8. Set aside.

  9. 9. Toss the carrots, and onions, 1 tablespoon of salt and 2 teaspoons of pepper in the fat in the pan and cook for 10 to 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onions are lightly browned.

  10. 10.Add the garlic and cook for 1 more minute.

  11. 11. Add the Cognac, stand back, and ignite with a match to burn off the alcohol.

  12. 12. Put the meat and bacon back into the pot with the juices.

  13. 13. Add the bottle of wine plus enough beef broth to almost cover the meat.

  14. 14. Add the tomato paste and thyme.

  15. 15. Bring to a simmer, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and place it in the oven for about 1 1/4 hours or until the meat and vegetables are very tender when pierced with a fork.

  16. 16. Combine 2 tablespoons of butter and the flour with a fork and stir into the stew.

  17. 17. Add the frozen onions.

  18. 18. Saute the mushrooms in 2 tablespoons of butter for 10 minutes until lightly browned and then add to the stew.

  19. 19. Bring the stew to a boil on top of the stove, then lower the heat and simmer for 15 minutes.

  20. 20. Season to taste.

  21. 21. Toast the bread in the toaster or oven.

  22. 22. Rub each slice on 1 side with a cut clove of garlic.

  23. 23. For each serving, spoon the stew over a slice of bread and sprinkle with parsley.

Wine Pairing

When thinking about wines to pair with Beef Bourguignon, Pinot Noir immediately comes to mind. However, since we are basically talking about beef stew, one can expand the list to include Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, or any other red wine than has sufficient tannins to counter the rich and tender stewed beef.

Beef Bourguignon is one of those dishes that begs the sommelier, whether domestic or professional, to use a wine that is easily found in the area of the dish’s origin.

Old world style, rustic Pinot Noir is my favorite wine to pair with this dish and it would seem that Burgundy should be my natural choice.

The Perfect Side Dish

Gratin dauphinois is a traditional regional French dish based on potatoes and crème fraîche, from the historic Dauphiné region in south-east France.

The gratin dauphinois is made with raw potatoes, thinly sliced, and cream, cooked in a buttered dish rubbed with garlic.

For 1 kg of potatoes, about 600 ml of cream, 25 g of butter and a clove of garlic are needed.

The potatoes are peeled and sliced to the thickness of a coin, preferably with a mandoline; they are layered in a shallow earthenware dish and cooked in a slow oven, at about 150°C, for more than an hour; the heat is raised for the last 10 minutes of the cooking time.
